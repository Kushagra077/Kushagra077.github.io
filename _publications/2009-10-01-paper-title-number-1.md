**Abstract:**  
This research work dives into an in-depth evaluation of the **YOLOv8 (You Only Look Once)** algorithm's efficiency in object detection, especially focusing on **Barcode and QR code recognition**. Utilizing the real-time detection abilities of YOLOv8, we performed a study aimed at enhancing its capability to swiftly and accurately identify objects. Through extensive training and fine-tuning on **Kaggle datasets** tailored for Barcode and QR code detection, our goal became to optimize YOLOv8's overall performance across various conditions and environments. 

The evaluation encompasses the assessment of YOLOv8 through different model iterations: **Nano, Small, and Medium**, with meticulous attention to precision, recall, and F1 assessment metrics. The results exhibit significant improvements in object detection accuracy with every subsequent model refinement. Specifically, we achieved an accuracy of **88.95%** for the Nano model, **97.10%** for the Small model, and **94.10%** for the Medium model, showcasing the incremental improvements achieved through model scaling. Our findings highlight the substantial strides made by YOLOv8 in pushing the limits of **computer vision**, ensuring its role as a milestone within the field of object detection. This study sheds light on how model scaling affects object recognition, enhancing the understanding of deep learning-based computer vision techniques.
